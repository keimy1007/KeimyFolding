Introduction

Welcome to Keimy Folding - your comprehensive platform for exploring the world of bioinformatics and protein analysis. We've designed this resource specifically for beginners and students who are taking their first steps into biological data analysis.

Our platform serves as a curated gateway to essential bioinformatics resources, bringing together the most important databases, analysis tools, and learning materials in one accessible location. Whether you're a student, researcher, or simply curious about biological data analysis, Keimy Folding provides you with a clear pathway to understanding and utilizing these vital resources.

Start your journey into bioinformatics with our carefully selected collection of tools and resources!

Databases

Essential database resources for biological data analysis.

Medical Datasets

  • Kermany Datasets https://data.mendeley.com/datasets/rscbjbr9sj/2

    A large collection of retinal OCT images for various eye conditions.

  • Harvard Glaucoma Datasets https://github.com/Harvard-Ophthalmology-AI-Lab/Harvard-GDP

    A comprehensive dataset for glaucoma diagnosis and progression analysis.

  • OASIS Datasets https://sites.wustl.edu/oasisbrains/

    Open Access Series of Imaging Studies for brain imaging.

  • APTOS 2019 https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/aptos2019-blindness-detection

    Dataset for detecting diabetic retinopathy.

  • DRIVE Dataset https://drive.grand-challenge.org/

    Digital Retinal Images for Vessel Extraction, used for vessel segmentation.

  • STARE Dataset https://cecas.clemson.edu/~ahoover/stare/

    Structured analysis of retina dataset for vessel segmentation and disease detection.

  • REFUGE Dataset https://refuge.grand-challenge.org/

    Retinal Fundus Glaucoma Challenge dataset for glaucoma detection.

Nucleotide Databases

  • DDBJ (DNA Data Bank of Japan) https://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/
  • ENA (European Nucleotide Archive) https://www.ebi.ac.uk/ena
  • GenBank https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/genbank/
